Logistics

Getting to Udupi

November's gonna be packed — flights and trains get booked soon. Sooooo we recommend you start booking your flights and trains ASAP as possible 👀

✈️

By Air

The nearest airport is Mangalore International Airport (IXE), about 60 km from Udupi.

🚂

By Train

Udupi Railway Station is ideal if you're travelling from Bangalore or Mumbai — scenic AF. Oh oh plus, Vande Bharat from Bangalore sooooo supes great views.

🚗

By Road

If you got a car, then drive down! The roads are great and it'll be easy for you to get around town.

Local Transport

Getting Around Udupi

Navigating around Udupi is pretty chill — if you don't have a car, it's fine BUT there's NO Uber, Rapido, Ola or Namma Yatri just FYI.

🛵

Scooter / Bike Rental

The prices are pretty reasonable if you're looking to travel around by yourself.

🚕

Autos & Cabs

No app-based rides, so you'll have to hail autos the old-fashioned way. However, they'll take you by the meter. For cabs, you can hire for the day. You can just let either of us know if you want to and we'll get you connected with the drivers.

🚌

Buses

Pretty easy to go around in these if you're planning to go to Mangalore or Manipal from Udupi. The drivers think they're F1 drivers, just in case y'all wanna experience that.

What to Pack

November in Udupi

Post monsoon, it's cooler than the regular summer months.

🌡️

Temperature

25–32°C during the day. Evenings drop to around 22°C.

🌦️

Rain Chance

Occasional showers but nothing major.

👗

What to Wear

Light cottons and linens for daytime. Comfortable footwear for temple visits (you'll be barefoot a lot).
And please wear traditional clothes (kurtis/sarees) if you plan to visit temples.

🕶️

Beach Days

Sunglasses, a hand fan if possible, and more sunscreen than you think you may need.

Need to Know

Practical Tips

A few things to keep in mind before you travel.

📅

Book Early!

November is peak season. Manipal kids have their convocation ceremony taking place then so places will get booked out!

🗣️

Language

Locals speak Tulu and Kannada. But y'all can speak English and Hindi — no one's gonna send you away…this isn't Bangalore.

👟

Temple Etiquette

Remove footwear at temples. Please pack a kurta/kurti just in case you feel devotional and plan a visit to the temple.
